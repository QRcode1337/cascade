High-Impact Outreach Scripts (Target 1: No Website)
📧 Email: The "Pattern Interrupt" (Anti-Spam)
This email is designed to look like a customer email, not a marketing blast. It is short, direct, and lowercased to feel personal.
Subject: I couldn't find your menu/website Alternate Subject: Your Google Maps button is broken
Body:
Hey [Owner Name],
I live in [Your Neighborhood] and tried to look up your services/menu on my phone yesterday, but the "Website" button on your Google Maps profile is empty.
I figured you were just busy, so I spent 20 minutes mocking up a simple mobile page for you.
It’s basically a "Digital Receptionist" that shows your info and lets people book/order without calling.
I put the demo here: [LINK TO TYPEBOT]
I’m not a marketing agency. I’m just a local automation guy. If you want to use this link permanently, let me know and I'll transfer it to you.
Best,
[Your Name] Sent from my iPhone
📞 Phone Script: The "Local Neighbor" Approach
The goal is NOT to sell. The goal is to get permission to text them the link.
You: "Hey, is this the owner?" Owner: "Yes, who is this?" You: "Hey, my name is [Your Name]. I’m actually a neighbor, I live over on [Street Name/Area]. I'm not trying to sell you SEO or anything weird."
(Pause - let them relax)
You: "I was looking you guys up on Google Maps yesterday, and I noticed your 'Website' button is empty. I know you're busy running the business, but you're probably missing out on a lot of 'easy' customers who just search on their iPhones." Owner: "Yeah, we haven't gotten around to it." You: "Totally get it. Look, I’m a tech guy. I actually built a little demo link for you yesterday just to see if I could fix it. It takes like 2 seconds to look at." You: "Can I text it to this number? You can just click it and see if you like it."
If Yes: Text the link immediately. If No: "No worries, just wanted to help out the neighborhood. Have a good one."
🎙️ Audio Script (Voicemail / LinkedIn Voice Note)
Keep this under 30 seconds. Sound casual, maybe even a little distracted. Do NOT sound like a radio announcer.
"Hey [Owner Name], it's [Your Name].
I'm a local over in [Neighborhood]. I was looking at your Google Maps profile just now and realized you don't have a website link listed.
I actually went ahead and built a little 'Digital Receptionist' link for you guys—it basically just answers questions and takes bookings so you don't have to answer the phone as much.
I'm going to text the link to this number. Take a look at it on your phone when you have a sec. If you want to keep it, just let me know. Talk soon."
📱 The "Follow-Up" Text (Send immediately after the call/voicemail)
Text: "Hey, this is [Your Name] (the neighbor who called). Here is that demo link I built for [Business Name]:
[LINK]
It acts like a mobile website. Let me know if you want to keep it live."
